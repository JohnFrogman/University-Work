About Me

On a Friday, around noontime as snow covered the ground in a fine white layer Alexander, or Alex as everyone calls him was born. After an almost completely normal and uneventful childhood beyond a broken arm at age five he moved on to study Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry and Biology at A level before applying to the University of Liverpool to study physics. Making it in he embarked upon a four year course, stopping only to catch his breath when his lungs collapsed.

Soldiering on through University in the winter and surgery through the summer he eventually wavered and deferred his study for a year, searching for less strenuous work and filling up the rest of his time with voluntary activities. It was in this year that he learnt some very rudimentary programming, though little could come from it, as it was soon time to return and finish his degree.

He came back to the third year, with no intention of attempting the fourth, he would do his dissertation here and now and be done with exams and assignments once and for all. From his free time away from complicated math problems he continued with a little programming, slowly developing a taste for it. After a meeting with the careers department he had made up his mind, if it was feasible then he would try and get a job as a software developer, he would learn on the job the subtleties of programming.

Unfortunately this wasn't to be, a lacklustre degree specification with a bare CV will do that. And after a great deal of searching and only, odd jobs and an unpaid shift as a kitchen porter to show for it Alex decided to return to school, a masters degree with an actually good mark would be taken seriously, more seriously than a bunch of half finished projects and participation in a solitary Gamejam. And so here he is now; writing this webpage for this module to the best of his abilities. Determined to do more than just pass.

Hobbies

Of course everyone has hobbies, and Alex is no different. People are more than their history and career so here mentioned in brief is some of the colour to the otherwise fairly blank canvass of Alex Hayes.

Alex is a very passionate video game enthusiast, from Super Mario World to Dwarf fortress he enjoys most of what the past-time has to offer. Video games truly have something to offer to everyone.

From one hobby grew another and, along with Sam Rowlands, he began working on making his own video games, you can see some of their work on Sam's site. You can even play their magnum opus ( made over the course of a weekend during a Gamejam, the only game they ever completed ) here. You'll need to install Unity webplayer.

Beyond that Alex holds a strong interest in reading. Previously a fan of mainly fantasy and science fiction, with China Miéville and Alastair Reynolds being two of his favourite authors he strayed recently in the works of the old masters. After seeing Les Misérables in London's West End, he decided to pick up Victor Hugo's famous novel. After months of working through the nine hundred page epic, Alex decided that there was a reason for these books being called classics before making short work of Bram Stoker's Dracula. After that he began what would become one of his favourite books, Dostoyevsky's Crime and Punishment.

Reading lends itself to writing and from an early age Alex has enjoyed conjuring up stories. Several of which have shown up recently, after having been locked away in a cupboard for more than a decade. This discovery inspired him to pick up the pen from where he left it over ten years ago.
